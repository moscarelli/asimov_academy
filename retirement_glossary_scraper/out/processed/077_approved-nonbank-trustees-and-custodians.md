# Approved Nonbank Trustees and Custodians
The IRS maintains a list of entities approved to serve as nonbank trustees or custodians.

## Background
Nonbank trustees or nonbank custodians (NBT) are described under Treasury Regulation Section 1.408-2(e). An entity that is not a bank (or an insurance company in the case of Archer Medical Savings Accounts and health savings accounts) can request to be a nonbank trustee/custodian by applying in writing and demonstrating that certain requirements will be met.

## Requirements for Approval
To qualify as a nonbank trustee or custodian, an entity must demonstrate compliance with the following requirements:

*   **Archer medical savings account (MSA)**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 408(m) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Health savings account**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 4980(b) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Qualified retirement plan custodial account**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 4979(a)(1) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **403(b)(7) Custodial account**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 403(b)(7) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Individual retirement arrangement (IRA)**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 408(a) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Roth IRA**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 408A of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Deferred compensation plan of state & local government custodial accounts**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 457(b)(2) of the Internal Revenue Code.
*   **Coverdell education savings account**: Demonstrates compliance with the requirements of Section 529(a) of the Internal Revenue Code.

## Application Procedure
To apply for approval as a nonbank trustee or custodian, an entity must submit an application to the IRS. The application must demonstrate compliance with the requirements outlined above.

### List and Application Updates

The IRS will update this list regularly as new entities are approved or removed from the list.

### Notification Requirements
An approved nonbank trustee or custodian is required to notify the IRS in writing of any changes that affect the accuracy of their representation on the list.
