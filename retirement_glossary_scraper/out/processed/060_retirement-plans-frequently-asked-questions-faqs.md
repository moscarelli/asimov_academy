# Retirement Plans Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## Overview

The IRS provides information on retirement plans, including types of plans, distributions, required minimum distributions, and more. This page contains frequently asked questions and answers about retirement plans.

## Types of Retirement Plans

* IRAs: Individual Retirement Accounts
* SIMPLE IRA Plans: Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees Individual Retirement Account
* SEPs: Simplified Employee Pension Plans
* 403(b) Tax-Sheltered Annuity Plans: A type of tax-deferred retirement plan for certain employees
* Designated Roth Accounts: A type of Roth IRA account
* SARSEPs: Savings and Individual Retirement Plan for Small Employers
* Multiple Employer Plans: A type of retirement plan that allows multiple employers to share the cost of providing a retirement plan

## Distributions (Withdrawals)

* Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs): The amount an individual must take from their retirement account each year starting at age 72
* Hardship Distributions: A withdrawal of funds from a retirement account due to a qualified hardship

## Rollovers

* Rollovers: The transfer of funds from one retirement account to another, such as from a former employer's plan to an individual IRA
* Waivers of the 60-Day Rollover Requirement: Exceptions to the rule requiring a 60-day waiting period before rolling over funds

## Loans and Form 8955-SSA

* Loans: The ability to borrow from a retirement account, subject to certain rules and penalties
* Form 8955-SSA: A notice that must be provided to plan participants when loans are made from their retirement plans

## Plan Investments and Termination of a Retirement Plan

* Plan Investments: The investments allowed in a retirement plan, such as stocks and bonds
* Termination of a Retirement Plan: The end of a retirement plan, which can result in the distribution of plan assets to participants

## Governmental Plans and EPCRS

* Governmental Plans: Retirement plans for government employees
* EPCRS: An employer-sponsored pension correction system

### Forms and Publications

The following forms and publications are related to retirement plans:

* Form 8955-SSA: IRS Filing Notices for Forms 5500, 5558 or 5500-EZ
* Form W-9: Request for Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) and Certification
* Form 1040-X: Amended Individual Income Tax Return

### Automatic Contribution Arrangements (ACA)

Automatic Contribution Arrangements (ACA) allow employers to automatically enroll employees in a retirement plan.

### Correcting Plan Errors

Correcting plan errors involves addressing mistakes or discrepancies in a retirement plan, such as underfunding or misallocating contributions.

### Self-Correction Program

The self-correction program is an IRS initiative that allows employers to correct certain errors in their retirement plans without penalty.
