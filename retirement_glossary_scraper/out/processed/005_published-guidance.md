# Published Guidance

## Overview

The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) provides guidance on various topics related to taxation, including individual and business tax information, refund and payment options, and more.

### How to File

- **Individuals:**
  - **How to file:** The IRS accepts tax returns by mail or online.
  - **When to file:** Tax returns are due on April 15th of each year.
  - **Where to file:** Return forms to the address listed in the instructions for the form.

## Tax Records and Payments

### Tax Record (Transcript)

- **Apply for a transcript:** Request a tax record transcript by mail or online.
- **Amend your return:** Use Form 1040-X to amend your individual income tax return.

### Payment Options

- **Bank Account (Direct Pay):** Make payments directly from your bank account using the Electronic Federal Tax Payment System (EFTPS).
- **Payment Plan (Installment Agreement):** Set up a payment plan to pay your taxes in installments.
- **Refund Direct Deposit:** Choose to have your refund deposited into your bank account.

## Credits and Deductions

### Earned Income Credit (EITC)

- **Application process:** Apply for the EITC on IRS Form 1040.
- **Eligibility requirements:** Meet specific income and family size requirements.

### Standard Deduction

- **Individual standard deduction:** Calculate your standard deduction based on your filing status.
- **Businesses & Self-Employed standard mileage:** Determine your standard business mileage rate.

## Retirement Plans

### Forms and Publications

- **Operate a retirement plan:** Use IRS publications to understand the rules and regulations for operating a retirement plan.
- **Actuarial tables:** Find monthly weighted-average interest rates that apply to your retirement plan using actuarial tables.

### Priority Guidance Plan

- **Current and prior versions:** Access current and prior versions of the Priority guidance plan.
- **News or notices:** Find news and notices about the Priority guidance plan on the IRS website.

## Tax Code, Regulations, and Official Guidance

### Sources of Authority

- **Tax code:** Search the tax code online for free to find authority for tax rules and procedures.
- **Regulations:** Consult the regulations for specific guidance on tax matters.
- **Official guidance:** Use the IRS website as a resource for official guidance on various topics.

## Penalties and Refunds

### Penalties

- **Interest and penalties:** Understand how interest and penalties apply to your taxes.

### Refunds

- **Where's My Refund?:** Find out where your refund is and when you can expect it.
- **Direct deposit:** Choose to have your refund deposited into your bank account.
