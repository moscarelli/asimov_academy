# Saving for Retirement

Saving for retirement is crucial to ensure a comfortable financial future after you stop working.

## Benefits of Saving Now

Benefits of saving now include:

*   Eligibility and participation in employer-sponsored retirement plans
*   Contributions types and limits
*   Investing plan assets, including participant-directed accounts and automatic enrollment
*   Automatic deductions from your salary into your retirement account unless you make a different election
*   Distributions, including withdrawing money from your account
*   Tax on early distributions before reaching age 59 ½

## Types of Retirement Plans

Types of retirement plans include:

*   Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)
*   Employer-sponsored plans, such as 401(k), SIMPLE IRA, and Roth IRA
*   Defined Benefit plans
*   Annuities

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

Required minimum distributions start at age 72. RMDs are the amount you must take from your retirement accounts each year.

### When Can a Retirement Plan Distribute Benefits?

A retirement plan can distribute benefits once you reach age 59 ½, unless you have a hardship distribution or are separated from service for a qualified reason.

## Tax on Early Distributions

Tax on early distributions depends on the type of retirement account and your income level.

*   IRA withdrawals before age 59 ½ may be subject to income tax
*   Roth IRA withdrawals are not subject to income tax
*   Excess contributions may be subject to penalties and interest
