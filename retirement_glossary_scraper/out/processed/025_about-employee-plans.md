# About Employee Plans

The Employee Plans office is responsible for enforcing the federal tax laws governing retirement plans. It oversees and regulates various aspects of employee benefits, including retirement plans.

## Types of Retirement Plans

There are several types of retirement plans, including:

*   Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)
*   Traditional and Roth IRAs
*   Employer-sponsored 401(k) and 403(b) plans
*   Annuity contracts
*   Pension plans

## Required Minimum Distributions

Employers must make required minimum distributions from retirement plans to employees starting at age 72. These distributions can be made in the form of regular payments or a lump sum payment.

## Published Guidance

The Employee Plans office publishes guidance on various aspects of retirement plan compliance, including:

*   Publication 575: Retirement Plans with Distributions to Beneficiaries
*   Publication 571: Taxable Distributions from Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)
*   Notice CP-96-A: Required Minimum Distributions from Pensions and Annuity Contracts

## Forms and Publications

The Employee Plans office publishes various forms and publications, including:

*   Form 5500: Annual Return of Employee Benefit Plan
*   Form 5498-R: Rollover Contribution Information (Individual Retirement Account)
*   Publication 590-A: Individual Retirement Account (IRA) Distributions
*   Notice CP-96-A: Required Minimum Distributions from Pensions and Annuity Contracts
