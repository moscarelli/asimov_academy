# A Plan Sponsor's Responsibilities

As an employer, you are responsible for keeping your employee retirement plan in compliance with the Internal Revenue Code.

## Plan Document Requirements

* The plan document must be written to comply with all requirements.
* It becomes part of your plan.

### Key Provisions

* When employees are eligible to participate
* Types and amounts of allowable contributions
* How employer contributions are divided among participants
* When participants are vested
* When and how benefits are paid

## Plan Administration Tasks

* Review the plan document for law changes and update it when needed.
* Apply the plan's terms for participation, contributions, and distributions.
* Give required notices to participants.
* File required forms and documents with the IRS or Department of Labor.
* Determine if testing is required and run it timely.
* Maintain records for participant accounts.
* Invest plan funds and review associated fees.

### Fiduciary Responsibilities

Learn about your fiduciary responsibilities as an employer.

## Plan Maintenance

* Correct the plan (if it becomes non-compliant) and pay any associated fees.
* Communicate with your pre-approved plan provider:
	+ Learn what fees you'll be charged
	+ Keep the opinion or advisory letter issued by the IRS for your pre-approved plan
	+ Promptly sign any plan amendments sent to you
	+ Send copies of plan amendments to your plan administrator
* Inform your provider of changes to your business, employees, or compensation.
* Communicate frequently with your plan service provider and/or payroll department:
	+ New hires, re-hires, terminations, and compensation changes
	+ Accurate payroll compensation amounts for each participant
	+ Census data for determining plan eligibility and benefit payments
	+ Data necessary to accurately identify highly compensated employees
	+ Plan terms for defining employee contributions, plan payments, and loans
* Keep up with ongoing plan maintenance responsibilities.
* Review your service provider's reports:
	+ Allocation report for possible contribution errors.
	+ Distribution report to ensure timely required minimum distributions.
