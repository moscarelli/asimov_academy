# Preapproved Retirement Plans
## Introduction
A pre-approved retirement plan is a plan sold to employers by a document provider, such as a financial institution or benefits practitioner. The document provider requests IRS approval of the plan to meet the requirements of Internal Revenue Code Sections 401, 403(a), or 403(b).

### Types of Pre-Approved Retirement Plans
The IRS recognizes two types of pre-approved plans:
*   **Standardized plans**: Safe harbor plans that provide a high level of assurance.
*   **Non-standardized plans**: Plans that adopt the flexibility of plans under the predecessor Volume Submitter program.

#### 403(b) Pre-Approved Plans
The IRS issues opinion letters for prototype IRAs and 403(b) pre-approved plans. These plans are designed to meet the requirements of Section 403(b) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### Plan Language Resources
The IRS provides plan language resources, including:
*   **Cumulative lists**: Listings of required modifications (LRM).
*   **Quality assurance bulletins (QABs)**: Guidance on plan language and administration.
*   **Revision history**: Records of changes to plan language over time.

#### Providers of Pre-Approved Plans
The IRS recognizes several providers of pre-approved plans, including:
*   Document providers responsible for developing the plan document.
*   Plan administrators who administer the plan after it is adopted by an employer.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
The IRS provides FAQs on various topics related to pre-approved retirement plans, including:
*   Determination, opinion, and advisory letter issues.
*   Procedures for pre-approved plans.
*   Tips for employers using pre-approved plans.

#### Remedial Amendment Cycles
The IRS has a system of remedial amendment cycles that require plan providers to submit applications for new or amended plans within a specific timeframe.
