# Determination, Opinion and Advisory Letters
## What is a determination letter?
A favorable determination letter is an official letter from the IRS that states your retirement plan meets certain requirements. This letter can help you avoid penalties for non-compliance.

### Scope and Benefit of a Letter
The scope of a determination letter is to ensure that your plan meets minimum standards set by the Internal Revenue Code (IRC). The benefit of a letter is to provide assurance that your plan is eligible for tax-exempt status, allowing you to avoid penalties for non-compliance.

### Amending or Updating a Plan
If your plan no longer meets the required standards, you can amend or update it. You must submit Form 5310 and follow specific procedures to make changes to your plan.

### Applying for a Letter
You can apply for a determination letter if you have an individually designed plan. The process involves submitting Form 5310 and providing supporting documentation.

### Pre-Approved Retirement Plans
There are pre-approved retirement plans that meet the required standards, such as IRC 403(b) and IRC 401(a). You can find a list of approved plans on the IRS website.

### Information for Adopting Employers and Document Providers
If you're an adopting employer or document provider, you must follow specific procedures to apply for a determination letter. The IRS provides guidance on this process.

### Check the Status of Your Letter
You can check the status of your determination letter application by following these steps:

1.  Go to the IRS website and search for Form 5310.
2.  Click on "Apply for a Determination Letter" and follow the instructions.
3.  Once you've submitted your application, you can check the status of your letter using the IRS's online portal.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
The IRS provides FAQs on determination, opinion or advisory letter issues to help answer common questions.
