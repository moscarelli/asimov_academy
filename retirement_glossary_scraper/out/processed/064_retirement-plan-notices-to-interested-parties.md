# Retirement Plan Notices to Interested Parties

## What is a Notice?

An employer must give notice to interested parties at least 10 days but no more than 24 days before submitting an application to the IRS for a determination of a retirement plan's qualified status.

### Who are Interested Parties?

* Present employees eligible to participate in the plan
* Other present employees with principal place of employment in the same location as those eligible to participate
* All present employees with accrued benefits under the plan
* Former employees with vested benefits under the plan
* Beneficiaries of deceased former employees currently receiving benefits

## What is Included in the Notice?

The notice must provide:

* Brief description of the class or classes of interested parties addressed
* Plan name, identification number, and administrator's name
* Applicant's name and taxpayer ID number
* Application type (initial qualification, plan amendment, termination, or partial termination)
* Description of eligible employees under the plan
* Previous IRS determination on qualified status
* Information for submitting comments to EP Determinations
* Specific dates for comments or requests
* Number of interested parties needed for Department of Labor comment

### Plan with Fewer than 26 Participants

If there are fewer than 26 participants, instead of providing additional information, the employer may provide a document containing:

* Plan requirements for participation and benefits
* Vesting provisions
* Circumstances resulting in ineligibility or loss/benefit denial
* Source of financing and provider identity
* Optional benefits reductions or eliminations
* Coverage schedule or demonstration
