# Retirement Topics - Catch-up Contributions

Individuals who are age 50 or over at the end of the calendar year can make annual catch-up contributions.

## Annual Catch-up Contribution Limits

- $7,500 in 2023 and 2024 (previously $6,500 in 2021-2020; $6,000 in 2015 - 2019)
- $23,000 in 2024 ($22,500 in 2023; $20,500 in 2022; $19,500 in 2020 and 2021; $19,000 in 2019)

## Types of Plans that Permit Catch-up Contributions

- 401(k) (other than a SIMPLE 401(k))
- 403(b)
- SARSEP
- governmental
- 457(b)
- Elective deferrals (not treated as catch-up contributions until exceeding $23,000 or ADP test limit)

## Catch-up Contribution Eligibility

A participant can make catch-up contributions for a year up to the lesser of:

- The catch-up contribution dollar limit
- The excess of the participant's compensation over the elective deferral contributions that are not catch-up contributions.

Plan participants must make catch-up contributions to a retirement plan via elective deferrals. Catch-up contributions must be made before the end of the plan year.

## Plan-Specific Catch-up Amounts

### SIMPLE IRA or SIMPLE 401(k)

- Annual catch-up contributions up to $3,500 in 2023 and $3,000 in 2015 - 2022
- Salary reduction contributions not treated as catch-up until exceeding $15,500 in 2023 ($14,000 in 2022; $13,500 in 2020 and 2021; and $13,000 in 2015 - 2019)

### 403(b) Plan

- Employees with at least 15 years of service eligible for additional contributions
- Regular catch-up contribution limits apply for participants age 50 or over

### IRA Catch-up Amounts

- Up to $1,000 in 2015 - 2023
