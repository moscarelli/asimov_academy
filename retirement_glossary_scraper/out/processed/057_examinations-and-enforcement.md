# Examinations and Enforcement

## Overview

The Employee Plans Examination (EP) program is responsible for overseeing compliance with the retirement plan provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. The EP examination process helps ensure that retirement plans operate in accordance with applicable laws.

### What We Do

*   Conduct audits to analyze operational features of retirement plans
*   Review procedures to help you find and prevent mistakes in administering your plan
*   Identify compliance risks and recurring plan issues

## Employee Plans Examination Process

The EP examination process is designed to ensure that retirement plans comply with the Internal Revenue Code.

### Steps in the Examination Process

1.  **Centralized Case Selection**: The IRS uses a centralized case selection and review process to enhance consistency of enforcement activities.
2.  **Examination Case Selection**: The IRS conducts audits to analyze operational features of retirement plans.
3.  **Audit Closing Agreement Program (Audit CAP)**: Plan errors found during an EP examination may be resolved through the Audit Closing Agreement Program.

## International Issues

The IRS is committed to addressing international tax compliance and abuse.

### Abusive Tax Transactions

Abusive tax avoidance transactions involving retirement plans undermine the confidence in our voluntary tax system.
