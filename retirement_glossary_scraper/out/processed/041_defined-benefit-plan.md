# Defined Benefit Plan

A defined benefit retirement plan provides a fixed, pre-established benefit for employees at retirement. Employees often value the fixed benefit provided by this type of plan.

## Key Features

* Provides a predictable benefit
* Vesting schedules can vary from immediate to spread out over seven years
* Benefits are not dependent on asset returns
* Can be used to promote certain business strategies by offering subsidized early retirement benefits

## Who Contributions

* Generally, the employer makes most contributions
* Sometimes, employee contributions are required or voluntary contributions may be permitted

## Contribution and Benefit Limits

* Benefits provided under the plan are limited
* Deduction limit is any amount up to the plan's unfunded current liability

## Filing Requirements

* Annual filing of Form 5500 is required
* An enrolled actuary must sign the Schedule B of Form 5500

## Additional Information

* Can have other retirement plans
* Can be a business of any size
* Must annually file a Form 5500 with a Schedule SB
* Need to have an enrolled actuary determine the funding levels and sign the Schedule SB
