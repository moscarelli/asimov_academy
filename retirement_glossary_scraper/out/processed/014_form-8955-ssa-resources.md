# Form 8955-SSA Resources
## Overview
Form 8955-SSA is used to provide information about retirement plans in Indian tribes. The form is used to comply with the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA) and the Internal Revenue Code.

### Filing Information Returns Electronically (FIRE)
The IRS requires certain employers to file Form 8955-SSA electronically through the Filing Information Returns Electronically (FIRE) system. This includes plan sponsors, investment companies, and other entities that have fiduciary duties under ERISA.

### Mandatory Electronic Filing
Mandatory electronic filing applies to certain returns, including Forms 5500, 5500-SR, 5500-EZ, and 8955-SSA. These returns must be filed electronically through the FIRE system by a specified deadline.

### Mail or Private Delivery Services
If you cannot file Form 8955-SSA electronically, you can mail it to the IRS address listed below:

* Department of the Treasury
* Internal Revenue Service Center
* Ogden, UT 84201-0024

Private delivery services should send Forms 5500 and 8955-SSA to:

* Internal Revenue Service
* 1973 North Rulon White Blvd.
* Ogden, UT 84404

### Frequently Asked Questions
For information on Form 8955-SSA, including FAQs for plan sponsors, investment companies, and other entities, visit the IRS website.

### Guidance
Announcement 2011-21 provides guidance on replacing Schedule SSA with Form 8955-SSA. For more information, refer to Publication 4810 or the Form 8955-SSA 2-D Barcode Standards document.

### Reporting Requirements
Plan sponsors and fiduciaries are required to report certain information about retirement plans, including pension plan assets, benefit payments, and plan sponsor liability.

### Penalties
Penalties may apply for failure to file Form 8955-SSA or other related returns. The IRS provides guidance on penalties and reporting requirements in the Publication 4810 document.
