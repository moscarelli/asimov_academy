# Retirement Topics - IRA Contribution Limits

## Overview

This page provides information on the IRS contribution limits for Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs).

### General Contribution Limits

For tax years 2025 and 2024:

* Traditional IRAs: $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50 or older)
* Roth IRAs: $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50 or older)

For tax year 2023:

* Traditional IRAS: $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older)
* Roth IRAS: $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older)

### Deduction Limits for Traditional IRAs

Taxable compensation exceeding certain levels may limit the deduction of traditional IRA contributions.

### Spousal IRAs

If filing a joint return, each spouse can contribute up to the current limit; however, total combined contributions cannot exceed taxable compensation reported on joint return.

### Examples

* Danny (unmarried college student): $3,500 contribution
* John (age 42): $6,000 contribution
