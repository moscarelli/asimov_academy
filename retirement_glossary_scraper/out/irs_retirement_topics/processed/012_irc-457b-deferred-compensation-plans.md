# IRC 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plans

## Introduction

IRC 457(b) deferred compensation plans are available for certain state and local governments and non-governmental entities tax exempt under IRC Section 501. These plans can be either eligible or ineligible under IRC 457(b) or 457(f), respectively.

## Who Can Establish a 457(b) Plan?

The organization must be a state or local government or a tax-exempt organization under IRC 501(c).

## How Do 457(b) Plans Work?

Employers or employees through salary reductions contribute up to the IRC 402(g) limit ($23,000 in 2024, $22,500 in 2023, and so on) on behalf of participants under the plan.

## Advantages of Participating in a 457(b) Plan

*   Contributions are tax-deferred.
*   Earnings on retirement money are tax-deferred.
*   A governmental 457(b) plan may be amended to allow designated Roth contributions and in-plan rollovers to designated Roth accounts.
