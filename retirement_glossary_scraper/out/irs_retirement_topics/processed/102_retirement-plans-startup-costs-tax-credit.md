# Retirement Plans Startup Costs Tax Credit
## Eligible Employers May Claim a Tax Credit of Up to $5,000

Eligible employers may claim a tax credit of up to $5,000 for three years for the ordinary and necessary costs of starting a SEP, SIMPLE IRA or qualified plan like a 401(k) plan.

### Qualifying Conditions
To qualify for this credit:

*   You had 100 or fewer employees who received at least $5,000 in compensation from you for the preceding year;
*   You had at least one plan participant who was a non-highly compensated employee (NHCE);
*   In the three tax years before the first year you're eligible for the credit, your employees werenâ€™t substantially the same employees who received contributions or accrued benefits in another plan sponsored by you, a member of a controlled group that includes you, or a predecessor of either.

### Amount of the Credit
The amount of the credit depends on the number of employees and the number of non-highly compensated employees (NHCEs):

*   If you have 50 or fewer employees who received at least $5,000:
    *   The credit is 100% of eligible startup costs, up to the greater of $500 or $5,000.
*   If you have 51-100 employees who received at least $5,000:
    *   The credit is 50% of your eligible startup costs, up to the greater of $500 or $5,000.

### Eligible Startup Costs
You may claim the credit for ordinary and necessary costs to:

*   Set up and administer the plan,
*   Educate your employees about the plan.

### Tax Credit for Plan Contributions

Small employers may claim a tax credit for plan contributions made to a defined contribution plan, SEP or SIMPLE IRA plan.
