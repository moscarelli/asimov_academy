# Types of Retirement Plans
## Overview

The IRS provides various types of retirement plans to help individuals save for their future. Here are some common types of retirement plans:

### 1. Individual Retirement Arrangements (IRAs)

*   Traditional IRA: Contributions are tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred.
*   Roth IRA: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and earnings grow tax-free.

### 2. Employer-Sponsored Plans

*   **401(k) plans**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan that allows employees to contribute a portion of their salary to the plan on a pre-tax basis.
*   **SIMPLE 401(k) plans**: A simplified version of traditional 401(k) plans, designed for small businesses and self-employed individuals.
*   **403(b) plans**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan for certain tax-exempt organizations, such as hospitals and schools.
*   **SEP plans (Simplified Employee Pension)**: A simple plan that allows employers to make tax-deductible contributions to employee retirement accounts.

### 3. Defined Benefit Plans

*   **Defined benefit pension plans**: These plans promise a certain benefit amount to employees in their retirement years, based on their salary and years of service.
*   **Money purchase plans**: Employer-funded plans that require the employer to make regular contributions to the plan.

### 4. Profit-Sharing Plans

*   **Profit-sharing plans**: Employer-sponsored plans that allow employers to contribute a portion of their profits to employee retirement accounts.

### 5. Governmental Plans

*   **457 plans**: A tax-deferred plan designed for employees of state and local governments, as well as tax-exempt organizations.
*   **Multiple employer plans (MEPs)**: Plans that cover employees of multiple employers who are in a business relationship with each other.

### 6. Other Retirement Plans

*   **Payroll deduction IRAs**: A type of IRA that allows employers to deduct contributions from employee paychecks and make tax-deductible contributions.
*   **Employee stock ownership plans (ESOPs)**: Employer-sponsored plans that allow employees to own shares of the company's stock.

### Choosing a Retirement Plan

When choosing a retirement plan, consider factors such as:

*   Fees
*   Investment options
*   Flexibility and portability
*   Employer contributions
*   Tax implications
