# Retirement Topics - 401(k) and Profit-Sharing Plan Contribution Limits
## Overview of Contribution Limits

Two annual limits apply to contributions:

*   A limit on employee elective salary deferrals.
*   An overall limit on contributions to a participant's account.

### Employee Elective Salary Deferrals

The limit on employee elective deferrals (for traditional and safe harbor plans) is:

*   $23,000 ($22,500 in 2023, $20,500 in 2022, $19,500 in 2021 and 2020; and $19,000 in 2019), subject to cost-of-living adjustments.

### Deferral Limits for SIMPLE 401(k) Plans

The limit on employee elective deferrals to a SIMPLE 401(k) plan is:

*   $16,000 ($15,500 in 2023, $14,000 in 2022, $13,500 in 2021 and 2020; and $13,000 in 2019)

### Catch-up Contributions

If permitted by the 401(k) plan, participants age 50 or over at the end of the calendar year can also make catch-up contributions. These amounts are subject to cost-of-living adjustments.

*   For traditional and safe harbor 401(k) plans: $7,500 in 2023 and 2024, $6,500 in 2022, 2021 and 2020, and $6,000 in 2019.
*   For SIMPLE 401(k) plans: $3,500 in 2023 and 2024, $3,000 in 2022 - 2015.

These amounts are subject to cost-of-living adjustments.
