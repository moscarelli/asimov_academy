## What is a Retirement Plan?

A retirement plan, also known as an employee benefit plan (EBP), is a type of plan that allows employers to provide retirement savings opportunities for their employees.

## Types of Retirement Plans

There are several types of retirement plans available:

*   **SEP (Simplified Employee Pension) plan**
*   **401(k) plan**
*   **SIMPLE (Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees) plan**

## How to Operate a Retirement Plan

To operate a retirement plan, you must:

1.  Determine the type of plan that is best for your business
2.  Establish eligibility requirements for participants
3.  Set up contribution arrangements
4.  Make required contributions and payments
5.  File annual reports with the IRS
6.  Provide certain benefits and information to participants

## Resources for Retirement Plan Operators

The following resources are available to help you operate a retirement plan:

*   **IRS Publication 5260 (Employer Shared Responsibility Payments):** Provides guidance on how to comply with employer shared responsibility payments.
*   **IRC Section 401(a) (Definition of Qualified Plan)::** Outlines the requirements for defining a qualified retirement plan.
*   **Regulations Governing Practice Before the IRS (Circular 230)**: Details the rules governing practice before the IRS, including those related to retirement plans.

### Forms and Publications

*   **Form 5500:** Requires annual reports on the retirement plan.
*   **Form W-9:** Required for participant contributions to a SEP or SIMPLE plan.
*   **Form W-2:** Used for distributions from a 401(k) or other qualified plan.
