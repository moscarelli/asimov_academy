# Payroll Deduction IRA
## Overview

A Payroll Deduction IRA (Individual Retirement Account) is a type of retirement plan that allows employees to contribute to an IRA through payroll deductions.

### Benefits of Saving Now

Saving now can help you get ready for a retirement that can last 30 years or more.

### How it Works

Under a Payroll Deduction IRA, an employee establishes an IRA (either a Traditional or a Roth IRA) with a financial institution and authorizes a payroll deduction for the IRA. The employer's responsibility is simply to transmit the employee's authorized deduction to the financial institution.

### Features of a Payroll Deduction IRA

*   Easy to set up and operate
*   Little administrative cost or requirements
*   Employee contributions may be eligible for the Saver’s Credit
*   No deduction for the business
*   May not create the same goodwill with employees as a 401(k) or other workplace retirement plan

### Who Contributes?

Employees make all contributions and control where their money is invested.

### Contribution Limits

Payroll Deduction IRAs have the same limits as other IRAs.

### Filing Requirements

Employer has no filing requirements.

### Participant Loans

IRA rules don’t permit loans or assets to be used as collateral.

### Withdrawals

Employee may withdraw contributions at any time, subject to income tax and 10% additional tax if under age 59½.
