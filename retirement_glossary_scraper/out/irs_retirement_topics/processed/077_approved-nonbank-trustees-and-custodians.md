# Approved Nonbank Trustees and Custodians
## Overview
The IRS maintains a list of entities approved to serve as nonbank trustees or custodians.

### Background
Nonbank trustees or nonbank custodians (NBT) are described under Treasury Regulation Section 1.408-2(e). An entity that is not a bank (or an insurance company in the case of Archer Medical Savings Accounts and health savings accounts) can request to be a nonbank trustee/custodian by applying in writing and demonstrating that certain requirements will be met.

### Requirements
To be approved as a nonbank trustee or custodian, an entity must meet the following requirements:

* Handle any of the following fiduciary accounts:
	+ Archer medical savings account (MSA)
	+ Health savings account
	+ Qualified retirement plan custodial account
	+ 403(b)(7) Custodial account
	+ Individual retirement arrangement (IRA)
	+ Roth IRA
	+ Deferred compensation plan of state & local government custodial accounts
	+ Coverdell education savings account

* Meet the requirements described in Treasury Regulation Sections 1.408-2(e)(2) - (e)(5)(viii)(F)

### Notification Procedure
An approved nonbank trustee or custodian must notify the IRS in writing of any change in entity name, address, or any other change that affects the continuing accuracy of any representation made in the application.
