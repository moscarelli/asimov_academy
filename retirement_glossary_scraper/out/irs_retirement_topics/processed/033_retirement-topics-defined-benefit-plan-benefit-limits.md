# Retirement topics - Defined benefit plan benefit limits

## Introduction

Contributions to a defined benefit plan are based on what is needed to provide definitely determinable benefits to plan participants. Actuarial assumptions and computations are required to figure these contributions.

## Benefit Limits

The annual benefit for a participant under a defined benefit plan cannot exceed the lesser of:

* 100% of the participant's average compensation for their highest 3 consecutive calendar years, or
* $275,000 for 2024 ($265,000 for 2023; $245,000 for 2022; $230,000 for 2021 and 2020; $225,000 for 2019)

The dollar amounts are subject to cost-of-living adjustments in future years.

## Publication

For more information, refer to Publication 560, Retirement Plans for Small Business (SEP, SIMPLE, and Qualified Plans).
